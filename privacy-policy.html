Privacy Policy

Last updated: 23 May 2026

Summary: We respect your privacy and are committed to protecting your personal data. This policy explains how we collect, use, and safeguard your information in compliance with the EU General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) and the German Telemedia Act (TMG).

1. Data Controller

The data controller responsible for processing your personal data is:

Aralel GmbH
Email: privacy@aralel.com

For any data protection questions, please contact us by email.

2. Data We Collect and How

2.1 Web Hosting and Server Log Files

When you visit our website, the web hosting provider automatically stores the following data in server log files:

  • IP address (anonymized or full, depending on configuration)
  • Date and time of access
  • Name and URL of the accessed file
  • Referring website (referrer URL)
  • Browser type and, where applicable, your device's operating system

Legal basis: Art. 6(1)(f) GDPR – legitimate interest in secure and uninterrupted operation of the website. Log data is deleted after at most 30 days.

2.2 Contact Form and Email

If you contact us via email or through the contact form on our website, we store your details (name, email address, message) to process your inquiry and for follow-up questions.

Legal basis: Art. 6(1)(b) GDPR (pre-contractual measures) or Art. 6(1)(f) GDPR (legitimate interest in responding to your inquiry). Data is deleted once your inquiry has been fully resolved.

3. Third-Party Services

3.1 Google AdSense

This website uses Google AdSense, a service for displaying advertisements provided by Google LLC, 1600 Amphitheatre Parkway, Mountain View, CA 94043, USA. Google AdSense uses cookies and web beacons to serve ads and measure their effectiveness. Information generated by these technologies (including your IP address) may be transmitted to and stored on Google's servers in the United States.

Google AdSense is only loaded after you have given your consent to marketing cookies via the cookie banner on this site. You may withdraw your consent at any time via the "Cookie Settings" link in the footer.

Legal basis: Art. 6(1)(a) GDPR – your consent. Without consent, Google AdSense is not loaded.

International transfer: Google LLC is based in the USA. The transfer is made on the basis of EU Standard Contractual Clauses (Art. 46(2)(c) GDPR). For more information, see https://policies.google.com/privacy.

3.2 Font Awesome (Cloudflare CDN)

This website uses Font Awesome icons served via Cloudflare's content delivery network (CDN). Cloudflare, Inc. is located at 101 Townsend St, San Francisco, CA 94107, USA. When loading these assets, your IP address and technical request data may be processed by Cloudflare.

Legal basis: Art. 6(1)(f) GDPR – legitimate interest in consistent and correct rendering of the website. Font Awesome is an essential part of the website's user interface.

International transfer: Cloudflare is based in the USA. The transfer is made on the basis of EU Standard Contractual Clauses. For more information, see https://www.cloudflare.com/privacypolicy/.

4.1 What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files stored on your device when you visit a website. We also use your browser's local storage (localStorage) for similar purposes.

4.2 Essential Cookies

These cookies are required for the website to function and cannot be disabled. They store your cookie preferences and session data.

With your consent, marketing cookies from Google AdSense are set to serve you personalized advertisements. These cookies may track your activity across multiple websites.

4.4 Managing Cookies

You can update your cookie preferences at any time by clicking Cookie Settings. You can also manage or delete cookies through your browser settings — note that this may affect the functionality of the website.

6. Right to Lodge a Complaint

Without prejudice to any other administrative or judicial remedy, you have the right to lodge a complaint with a supervisory authority if you believe that the processing of your personal data infringes the GDPR (Art. 77 GDPR). The competent supervisory authority is that of the German state where our company is registered, or the authority where you are located.

A list of German data protection supervisory authorities is available from the Federal Commissioner for Data Protection and Freedom of Information (BfDI): www.bfdi.bund.de.
